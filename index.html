The Classic Pina Colada

We don't know who created the Pina Colada but we're glad that they did! The first printed reference to the Pina Colada cocktail was found in 1906 issue of the Washington Post, dubbed 'Pina Fria' (cold pineapple) as it didn't contain rum or coconut milk. In 1954 someone has the great idea to combine coconut milk, rum and pineapple, creating the holiday classic we all know and love.

Difficulty

This is an easy one. Grab all the ingredients and let's get started.

Required

  • A cocktail glass or hollowed out pineapple if you're feeling extra fruity
  • 50ml of pineapple juice
  • 45ml of white rum
  • 50ml of coconut cream
  • Two triangles of pineapple

Step by Step

  1. Mix all the liquid ingredients in a shaker and add ice.
  2. Shake vigorously until a consistent texture
  3. Strain the concoction into the glass, or half a hollowed out pineapple for the full beach-holiday experience.
  4. Make a slit down the middle of both pineapple chunks and wedge on the rim of the glass
  5. Dig out a deck chair, chill to the sound of some Caribbean music and savour the tropical flavour of the classic.
